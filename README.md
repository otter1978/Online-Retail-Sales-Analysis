Online Retail Sales Analysis
Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing transactional e-commerce data to identify key sales trends, customer behavior patterns, and revenue drivers. The analysis was performed using PostgreSQL, while business insights were visualized in Power BI.
Project Goals
•	Analyze sales performance over time
•	Identify top customers and revenue contributors
•	Discover the best-performing products
•	Compare sales performance across countries
•	Generate business recommendations based on the analysis
Tools Used
•	PostgreSQL — data cleaning and analysis
•	Power BI — dashboard and data visualization
•	Microsoft Excel — initial data preparation
Dataset
Dataset: Online Retail II
The dataset contains transactional data from an online retail store, including:
•	invoices
•	products
•	quantities
•	prices
•	customers
•	countries
•	transaction dates
Key Insights
Sales Trends
•	Sales show strong seasonality, with a significant increase in September–November.
•	Revenue in November is 259% higher than in February and 169% above average.
Business Recommendation
•	Increase inventory and marketing activity before peak months.
•	Prepare logistics and staffing capacity for higher seasonal demand.
Customer Analysis
•	The two most active customers (12748 and 14911) place twice as many orders as the third-ranked customer.
•	The top 10 customers account for approximately 6% of all orders and 8% of total revenue.
•	High-frequency customers are not always the highest revenue generators.
Business Recommendation
•	Introduce VIP and loyalty programs.
•	Use personalized offers and retention campaigns
•	Segment customers into:
high-frequency
high-value
→ and target them differently
Product Performance
•	The most frequently sold products are not always the products generating the highest revenue.
•	The top 10 products account for approximately 10% of total revenue.
Business Recommendation
•	Focus marketing efforts on high-revenue products.
•	Use cross-selling and bundling strategies.
•	Optimize pricing strategy based on product performance
Geographic Insights
•	The UK generates 82% of total revenue.
•	Revenue from the UK is 25 times higher than the second-largest market.
Business Recommendation
•	Maintain strong focus on the UK market.
•	Explore expansion opportunities in underperforming regions.
•	Test localized marketing strategies outside the UK to diversify risk
Order Value
•	Average order value equals 480.76.
Business Recommendation
•	Increase basket size using upselling and promotional bundles.
